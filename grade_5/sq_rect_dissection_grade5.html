Square & Rectangle Dissection

Grade 5  ·  Geometry  ·  Feasibility, minimum sizes, and optimal splits

Feasibility Rectangles Min Side N Min Rect Optimize
  • Square impossible values: k = 2, 3, 5 are impossible for any square. k = 4 and all k ≥ 6 are possible.
  • +3 trick: from any valid k-split, replace one small square with 4 even smaller ones → a (k+3)-split.
  • Rectangle minimums: 1:2 → min k = 2  |  2:3 → min k = 3  |  3:4 → min k = 4.
  • Integer area formula: if using 2×2 and 1×1 pieces in an N×N square: 3p = N² − k (p = number of 2×2 pieces). Must be a non-negative integer.
  • Min N table: k=4→N=2  |  k=6,9→N=3  |  k=7,8,10→N=4  |  k=11→N=5.
  • Level 5 fractions: largest piece as fraction of A = (piece side) ÷ N. E.g. k=6: N=3, largest=2×2, fraction = (2/3)A.
