Mean, Median & Mode

Grade 5  ·  Mode, Median, Mean vs Median, Outlier Effects, Work Backwards, and Choosing the Right Measure

Mode Median Compare Outliers Backwards Choose
  • Mode: most frequent value. Two values tie → both are modes (bimodal). All values different → no mode.
  • Median: sort values first. Odd count → middle value. Even count → (lower middle + upper middle) ÷ 2.
  • Mean: Sum ÷ Count.
  • Outlier effect: extreme values pull the mean far from the middle but barely move the median. Median is more robust.
  • Work backwards (mean): missing = target × N − sum of known values.
  • Work backwards (even-count median): missing = 2 × target median − its sorted neighbour.
  • Choose: mean for balanced data, median for skewed/outliers, mode for categories.
