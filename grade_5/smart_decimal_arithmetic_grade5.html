Smart Decimal Arithmetic

Grade 5  ·  Learn to spot hidden structure in decimal expressions and calculate faster using algebraic thinking.

Why these tricks work

Every technique here is just the distributive law in disguise: a × b + a × c = a × (b + c). The skill is seeing which numbers share a hidden common factor.

The 5 strategies

  • L1 — Common Base Factor: Numbers like 201.4 and 2014 and 20140 are all multiples of the same base. Factor it out.
  • L2 — Complement to a Round Number: Add the "missing piece" to reach a nice multiple of 10 or 100, then subtract it again.
  • L3 — Two-Level Regrouping: Reorder three terms to reveal two hidden common factors one after the other.
  • L4 — Ratio Recognition: Two numbers may look unrelated but share a simple ratio (e.g., 3.75 = 3 × 1.25). Factor out the smaller one.
  • L5 — Algebraic Substitution: Name a repeated group as a variable, simplify algebraically, then substitute back.

Golden rule

Spend 5 seconds looking for a pattern. If you don't see one — just calculate directly. Never waste time forcing a trick that isn't there.
