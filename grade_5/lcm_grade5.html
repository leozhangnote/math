Least Common Multiple (LCM)

Grade 5  ·  Multiples, listing method, prime factorization, GCD shortcut, and word problems

Multiples Listing Prime Fact. GCD Shortcut Word Problems Properties

Quick Reference

  • Multiple: n × k for any positive integer k. Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, …
  • LCM by listing: list multiples of each number; the first value appearing in both lists is the LCM.
  • LCM by prime factorization: factor each number; for every prime that appears in either factorization, take the maximum exponent; multiply.
  • GCD shortcut: LCM(a, b) = a × b ÷ GCD(a, b). Find GCD first, then divide the product.
  • Word problem signal: "next time both coincide / meet again / synchronize" → LCM.
  • Properties: LCM(a, b) × GCD(a, b) = a × b. If a divides b, then LCM(a, b) = b. LCM(n, n) = n.
