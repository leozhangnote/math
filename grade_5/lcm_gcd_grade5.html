LCM & GCD Combined

Grade 5  ·  Competition  ·  Identity, pair reconstruction, three-number operations, word problems, and ratio problems

Identity Find Pair 3-Numbers Word Probs Relations Competition
  • Identity: LCM(a, b) × GCD(a, b) = a × b. Always.
  • Find the pair: GCD = g, LCM = L → write a = g·m, b = g·n where gcd(m, n) = 1 and m·n = L ÷ g. Each coprime factorisation of L÷g gives one valid pair.
  • Three numbers: GCD(a, b, c) = GCD(GCD(a, b), c). LCM(a, b, c) = LCM(LCM(a, b), c). Always reduce two at a time.
  • Ratio rule: If a : b = m : n with gcd(m, n) = 1, then GCD(a, b) = a÷m = b÷n and LCM(a, b) = m·n·GCD.
  • Word problem signals: "equal distribution / no waste" → GCD. "next coincidence / synchronise" → LCM.
  • LCM ÷ GCD: LCM(a,b) ÷ GCD(a,b) = (a÷GCD) × (b÷GCD) = the product of the coprime quotients.
