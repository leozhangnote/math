Smart Whole Number Arithmetic

Grade 4  ·  Learn to calculate whole numbers faster by spotting hidden patterns — no written working needed!

Common Factor Complement Method Ratio Shortcut Factor Splitting

Why these tricks work

Every technique here is the distributive law in action: a × b + a × c = a × (b + c). The skill is seeing which numbers share a hidden connection.

The 5 strategies

  • L1 — Common Factor: When every term is multiplied by the same number, factor it out and add/subtract the remaining parts first.
  • L2 — Complement to Round Number: Replace 99 with (100 − 1), or 98 with (100 − 2). Then check if the correction term already appears in the expression.
  • L3 — Two-Level Regrouping: In three-term expressions, group two terms to find a common factor — then factor again.
  • L4 — Ratio Shortcut: Two numbers may be secretly related (e.g., 75 = 3 × 25). Factor out the smaller one.
  • L5 — Factor Splitting: Break one awkward number into two friendly factors (e.g., 24 = 8 × 3 so 125 × 24 = 125 × 8 × 3 = 1000 × 3).

Key friendly pairs to remember

  • 125 × 8 = 1000  |  25 × 4 = 100  |  5 × 20 = 100  |  2 × 50 = 100

Golden rule

Spend a few seconds scanning for a pattern. If you don't see one — just calculate. Never force a trick that isn't there.
