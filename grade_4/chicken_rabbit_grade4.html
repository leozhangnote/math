Chicken-Rabbit Problems

Grade 4  ·  Solve "two unknowns" problems using the assumption method

Guess & Adjust Assume Small Assume Large Leg-Raising Three Types Strategy

Core Formulas

  • Assume all small: gap = actual total − small×heads; count of large = gap ÷ (large per − small per)
  • Assume all large: excess = large×heads − actual total; count of small = excess ÷ (large per − small per)
  • Leg-raising: remaining = total − small×heads; count of large = remaining ÷ (large per − small per)
  • Three types: subtract the known type first, then solve as a two-type problem
  • Verify always: count₁ + count₂ = total heads AND per₁×count₁ + per₂×count₂ = total quantity

Common Contexts

  • Chickens (2 legs) & Rabbits (4 legs)
  • Bicycles (2 wheels) & Tricycles (3 wheels)
  • ¥2 notes & ¥5 notes  |  Child tickets & Adult tickets
  • Small trucks (3 t) & Large trucks (5 t)
